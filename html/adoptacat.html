So you're interested in owning a cat? While the cat vs dog debate continues to be argued, there's no question that cats are easier to take care of, are less of a nuisance, are less expensive, and overall adorable. The comedians of the pet world, the playful and curious antics of the cats will always have you laughing. Even simple things such as a cardboard box or scrunched up paper ball can provide hours of amusement. If you are out all day at work or live in an apartment, cats could be perfect for you as they can look after themselves and are perfectly suited to an apartment or unit. Many of the cats that are available for adoption at Hornsby Pet Shelter, will have to be indoor pets for the rest of their lives due to FIV or cat flu. There are also meticulously clean and spend a lot of time grooming themselves and figure out how to use a litter box fairly quickly.

Despite the misunderstanding that all cats are aloof and unaffectionate, all cats have different personalities and traits. Each individual is full of character. There are cats suited to the hustle of family life and also those who prefer a peaceful life.It isn't only dogs that can be trained as well. Cats are very intelligent and through positive reinforcement training and some tasty treats they can be taught to come, sit, play fetch and other things.

How to Look After a Cat

There are many steps that you have to take to ensure that your cat has a comfortable environment and is cared for properly. These requirements are:

  • Provide plenty of human companionship
  • Provide regular, suitable meals with a constant supply of fresh water
  • Provide a clean and comfortable bed
  • Provide the cat with outdoor access or be prepared to empty and clean a litter tray on a daily basis
  • Provide it with a stimulating and safe environment
  • Groom it regularly
  • Vaccinate against the major feline diseases regularly
  • Worm regularly and provide treatment for fleas
  • Take the cat to the vet when it shows any sign of illness

All of the different conditions listed are required to make sure that your cat can live comfortably, healthily and safely. Though they are applicable to the majority of cats, they will differ between individuals and we highly recommend contacting the Hornsby Pet Shelter team members to ask for advice. Some of these specifications are quite broad, for example, how regularly cats should be groomed. It is recommended to groom short haired cats around once a week while medium or long haired cats should be groomed daily. This is one of the benefits of short-haired cats. Other conditions such as diet, creating a stimulating environment and when to take it to the vet are more complicated. While they can't be briefly summarized, some of the following questions adress how to take care of pets and what to provide for them.

Commonly asked questions

How much care and affection do cats need?

Cats require much more attention than the average person may expect. Cats, while often independent creatures, still crave attention and love, in addition to their obvious nutritional needs of fresh water, clean litter, and food. If you decide to bring a cat or kitten into your home, you should be prepared to spend at least 20 minutes a day giving your cat loving one-on-one attention. Obviously, each cat is going to have its own unique personality, and some may prefer to spend hours alone, while others may have a stronger desire to play and spend even more time with its owners. This can also depend on the breed of the cat.

Cats will often show you signs that they are not getting the love and attention they need in several different ways. This could include pawing at your legs or arms or constantly coming to you. When cats continue to not receive enough attention they could resort to more negative activities such as scratching furniture. If you do not have enough time or do not want to spend time with your cat it may be a better idea to adopt a quieter and less needy cat. Often older cats do not require as much attention.

What should I feed my cat?

Cats have a number of special dietary requirements that do not apply to other animals because they have adapted to become strict carnivores and do not have the metabolic pathways to digest and convert nutrients in plants. Cats can be fed many different types of meat such as cooked beef, chicken, turkey, and small amounts of lean deli meats, though raw or spoiled meat could make your cat sick. It is actually extremely difficult to provide a well-balanced diet for cats with home-prepared foods. Feeding a good quality commercial cat food (tins, sachets or dry food) is therefore preferable, at least as the major part of the diet. One of the most important things to look out for when choosing a food to feed your cat is that it is a â€˜completeâ€™ food (this will be stated on the packaging). This means that it has been developed to meet all of your catâ€™s nutritional needs. In addition, cats should never be fed dog food.

As for the frequency of feeding, naturally cats hunted prey that was small in size, such as mice, and would have to eat multiple portions a day. If cats are restricted to eating two or three big meals a day for the convenience of their owners it is behaviourally and physiologically unnatural for them. If food is unrestricted and available at all times it can cause them to overeat and become obese. If food is left in the same location it can also cause anxiety and frustration in cats as mental and physical exertion isn't needed. The best way to feed cats is a rotation of around 5 portions a day and using puzzle or timed feeders. Puzzle feeders are objects that must be manipulated by the cat to release food. This increases the physical exertion needed from the cat.

Moden conditions have made it more difficult for cats to be fed a healthy amount of food. A catâ€™s ability to regulate their calorie intake is often interfered with in a number of ways. Modern pet food is usually developed to be very tasty, which may lead to excessive food intake. Commercial cat food is also very easy to eat as it is presented in small chunks or bite-sized biscuits, meaning that cats can eat the food very quickly, which may result in overeating. Cats without sufficient enrichment or outdoor access may show increased attention to food through boredom. Competition for food due to the presence of other cats may lead to overeating. For cats who only need to walk to their bowl for food, it is more likely that the calories they take in through eating will outweigh the calories they use up through exercise, resulting in weight gain. Additionally, most pet cats are neutered, and while this has many health and welfare benefits for the cat and reduces the number of unwanted cats, it does interfere with their natural ability to regulate calorie intake and they will tend to consume more than is required.

To prevent overeating, try to control your cats food intake as much as possible. Make sure that you follow the guidelines on food packaging for how much your cat should eat and match this to their size. Dry food is very energy dense, so the correct amount may look small, but provides all the calories a cat needs to stay fit and healthy. Many owners can't tell what a 'healthy' weight or size for their cat is so it is encouraged that they ask their vet to determine their body condition score regularly.

Is there a type of cat that doesn't hunt?

You may have a great aversion to your cat hunting outside. Perhaps you are a bird lover, or are simply unable to deal with small carcases on the floor. Hunting is normal behaviour for cats. Keeping a cat indoors may prevent it actually killing anything, but it will still need an outlet for this, its most instinctive behaviour, and not all cats will be happy with an indoor lifestyle. If you have other pets there should not be a problem in getting a cat, but you just need to make sure that you take everything into consideration. If you have a dog you just need to make sure that you make introductions carefully so that your new cat is not chased or injured while the dog gets used to it. Not all dog types make good companions for cats and not all cats are good with dogs. What equipment should be provided for a cat?
  1. Kitty litter: this is a necessity for owning a cat. Thereâ€™s a wide range of options available. A litter box can be as simple as a plain plastic tray. Whatever works best for your budget and preferences will be fine.
  2. Bedding: cats like to have a place to call their own for sleeping. When youâ€™re shopping for a bed for your cat, youâ€™ll find yourself faced with a huge array of choices. Whatever you buy should be soft and warm. It might be as simple as a pillow, but lots of cats show a preference for nest-type beds (usually shaped somewhat like a donut).
  3. Scratching post: It is a part of cats nature to scratch, and this is something that can't be trained out of them as it is instinctive. To stop cats from damaging your property, a scratching post is needed. They should be sturdy and stable and as tall as your cats reach when it is standing on its hind legs.
  4. Food and water bowls: stainless steel bowls are normally prefered over plastic, as are glass and ceramic. Plastic bowls are the most likely to be hosts for harmful bacteria and to retain repellent smells. Puzzle or timed feeders are also a great option to give your cat food.
  5. Grooming equipment: at a minimum you want a wire brush, a metal-tooth comb (that doesn't have sharp teeth that could injure the cat) and a slicker brush. This is particularly important for long or medium haired cats. A deshedder tool is also useful.
  6. Coller: it is a good idea to own a collar for your cat. This should have break away features to prevent your cat from being stuck if the collar becomes snagged on anything. WWith an identification tag on it this will allow people to identify and return your cat if it becomes lost. If the cat has a habit of hunting animals that you wish to control, you can also attach a bell to the collar which will warn other animals when it gets close.
  7. Cat carrier: this is the only safe way to transport your cat. Even if you aren't planning any trips longer than to the vet, having a loose cat in the car is dangerous for both passengers and the animal. The carrier should be well ventilated and sturdy.
  8. Toys: cats are playful creatures and toys are very important to keeping them content and healthy. There are a wide range of different cat toys. You should make sure that the cat can't get any small pieces off the toy and anything smaller than a ping pong could potentially be a choking hazard.
Should I get an adult cat or a kitten?

A kitten gives you the opportunity to take on an animal right from the beginning and treat it and care for it so that it gets the best start in life. You will also be able to get some idea of its character. However, kittens require a lot of attention and some forethought to prevent them from getting into trouble. If you leave them alone you have to make sure they will be safe while you are away. While kittens have a huge â€˜cuteâ€™ factor, it is worth remembering that they donâ€™t stay kittens for very long â€“ just six months out of a potential 14 years or more. The age of the kitten or cat you choose to adopt really depends on what you are looking for!

What are the different breeds of cats?

There are many different breeds of cats, though almost all of the cats at shelters are 'moggies' or domestic short or long haired cats. These are a mixture of lots of different breeds of cats and often their parentage is unknown. If you are looking for a pedigree cat it would be better to go to a breeder.

How many cats can I have together?

When having multiple cats together, there may be a great deal of tension between them that owners just donâ€™t pick up on. Cats originate from a largely solitary species and although they can live in groups these are usually related individuals or are self-selected so that cats are not sharing space with cats that they donâ€™t get on with. Cats may start to spray or soil in the house because theyâ€™re trying to deal with a situation where they feel under stress because of other cats, and this might be all that owners notice. If you have two cats living together very successfully then think very carefully before you add more. If you have three cats living well together then thank your stars and quit while youâ€™re ahead!

The trouble with adding more is that it might not be just the relationship between the resident cats and the new one that causes problems; it may upset the whole equilibrium of the resident catsâ€™ relationship and introduce difficulties even between the original cats as tension and stress levels rise. The best way to have two compatible cats is to choose siblings. These will have grown up together, and this usually bodes well for a good future relationship (although never guaranteed!).

Can I have a cat with a baby or young children?

For the most part, there is no reason not to have a cat or kitten if you have children. It is up to parents to teach their children how to approach, stroke and handle cats and to treat them kindly. Many children have fantastic relationships with theircats and learn about respecting other creatures. Perhaps taking on a new kitten when you have a baby or a toddler might be a lot to handle at once, so you may choose a older cat. There are some cases with shelter cats in which they are not suited to living with young children. If they are more suited to living in a home without young children it will be mentioned in their information.
